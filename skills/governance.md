# Governance Skill

## Purpose

Use this skill when decisions affect authority, privacy, safety, fairness, consent, recognition, or community trust.

## Governance principle

> **Agentic Education is not governed by AI. It is governed by human communities using AI responsibly.**

## Decision rights

| Decision | Final authority |
|---|---|
| Learning quality in a classroom | Teacher / authorized educator |
| Child consent and dignity | Parent/family and applicable policy |
| Data collection and sharing | School/district policy and authorized humans |
| Recognition or credential issuance | Authorized human issuer |
| System architecture | Named project steward with documented review |
| AI recommendation | Never final by itself |

## Governance checks

Before a material decision, ask:

1. Who benefits?
2. Who could be harmed or excluded?
3. Whose consent is needed?
4. What data is necessary—and what can be avoided?
5. Is this decision reversible?
6. Who can appeal or correct it?
7. What will be documented?
8. What human has final authority?

## Trust before scale

When scalability conflicts with trust, choose trust.

When automation conflicts with teacher judgment, choose teacher judgment.

When speed conflicts with evidence, choose evidence.

## Red lines

- No automatic grading or credential issuance.
- No financialized student tokens.
- No public student data.
- No hidden surveillance design.
- No claims of AI objectivity without validation.
- No use of AI to override human safety or dignity concerns.
