# Research Skill

## Purpose

Use this skill for research questions, literature maps, evidence synthesis, grant drafts, evaluation protocols, and technical/educational reports.

## Research stance

OpenLab is a hypothesis-driven, teacher-informed project. Do not present aspirations as proven findings.

## Research workflow

```text
Question → Scope → Sources → Synthesis → Method → Evaluation → Limitations → Human Review → Report
```

## Required distinctions

Label statements as one of:
- **Source-backed fact**
- **Inference**
- **Design proposal**
- **Open question**
- **Assumption**
- **Pilot observation**

## Research brief requirements

Every research brief should contain:
- problem statement;
- research question(s);
- context and audience;
- source inventory;
- method or proposal;
- evaluation plan;
- limitations;
- privacy considerations;
- next decision.

## Grant-writing rules

- Lead with a real educational or research problem.
- Make the human-in-the-loop design explicit.
- State what will be measured.
- Explain what will not be automated.
- Use de-identified or synthetic artifacts for public work.
- Avoid overstating partnerships, institutional approval, or results.

## Suggested OpenLab research themes

- AI-supported rubric alignment;
- quality of learner reflection;
- teacher confidence and time burden;
- evidence completeness;
- student agency;
- privacy-preserving portfolio systems;
- human-AI collaboration provenance;
- trust and community validation;
- error analysis in AI recommendations.

## Citation discipline

- Cite original sources when possible.
- Do not invent citations or quotations.
- Separate source summaries from project interpretation.
- Record full bibliographic details in a durable source file.
