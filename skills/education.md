# Education Skill

## Purpose

Use this skill when designing learning experiences, missions, reflection prompts, teacher workflows, standards alignment, or human-AI collaboration in education.

## Working definition

**Agentic Education** is a learning model in which learners use AI responsibly to plan, build, test, explain, reflect, and improve—while human purpose, teacher judgment, evidence, and community trust remain central.

## Role model

| Role | Primary responsibility |
|---|---|
| Learner | Builds, reflects, revises, explains choices |
| Teacher | Coaches, validates, contextualizes, protects quality |
| AI | Supports inquiry, feedback, organization, simulation, drafting |
| Parent/family | Supports consent, dignity, values, and trust |
| School/district | Sets policy, access, safety, and equity conditions |
| Community | Provides authentic audience and contextual validation |

## Mission design rules

A strong mission includes:
- a real-world question or challenge;
- learner choice;
- an observable artifact;
- evidence of process;
- reflection;
- revision;
- a clear human reviewer;
- privacy-safe data handling.

## Learning loop

```text
Mission → Build → Evidence → Reflection → Feedback → Improve → Human Validation → Portfolio
```

## AI use rules in learning

AI can:
- generate options;
- ask questions;
- provide a scaffold;
- simulate;
- help organize;
- help translate or draft;
- help document a process.

AI should not:
- complete the core thinking in a way the learner cannot explain;
- give a final grade;
- fabricate evidence;
- bypass teacher review;
- pressure learners to share private information.

## Required student reflection prompts

1. What was your goal?
2. What did you make or test?
3. What evidence supports your claim?
4. Where did AI help, if AI was used?
5. What did you decide yourself?
6. What changed after feedback?
7. What would you do next?
