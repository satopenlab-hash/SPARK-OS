# Recognition Skill

## Purpose

Use this skill when designing evidence maps, badges, portfolios, validation records, or credential recommendations.

## Recognition principle

Recognition must reflect demonstrated learning, not merely completed output.

> The artifact is not the proof. The transformation is part of the proof.

## Evidence levels

| Level | Evidence |
|---|---|
| 1 | Claim only |
| 2 | Artifact |
| 3 | Artifact + learner reflection |
| 4 | Artifact + reflection + validation |
| 5 | Portfolio + community impact + longitudinal growth |

## Minimum recognition record

```text
Recognition title:
Learner or project identifier:
Criteria:
Evidence links:
Reflection link:
Reviewer:
Decision:
Date:
Confidence / limitations:
Privacy classification:
```

## Badge rules

A badge may be:
- a non-financial recognition record;
- a summary of validated evidence;
- portable metadata;
- a human-issued acknowledgement.

A badge must not be:
- a substitute for evidence;
- a tradable financial instrument;
- automatically issued by AI;
- a public record of private student data.

## AI role

AI may:
- organize evidence;
- flag missing criteria;
- draft a recommendation;
- create accessible portfolio summaries;
- prepare metadata.

AI must not:
- certify, grade, or issue final recognition alone.

## Verification prompt

Before recommending recognition, ask:

1. Is there a clear criterion?
2. Is there direct evidence?
3. Is learner reflection present?
4. Has an authorized human reviewed it?
5. Is the record privacy-safe?
6. What is the limitation of this recognition?
